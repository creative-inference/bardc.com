Joost Klein and Big G Own This Monday Night

Forget the Monday blues; D.C.’s nightlife scene is throwing everything from Dutch pop to Go-Go birthday bashes tonight. Whether you’re looking for a sweaty dance floor or some classic 90s covers, the District is surprisingly loud for a weeknight.

It’s mid-May, the humidity hasn't quite reached "swamp hell" levels yet, and for some reason, the booking gods have blessed this particular Monday with a weekend-level intensity. Usually, Mondays in the District are for laundry and regret, but tonight, the city is demanding you put on your walking shoes and probably some earplugs. From the Navy Yard to the heart of U Street, the energy is electric, proving that you don't need a Friday clearance to lose your mind on a dance floor.

The big story tonight is happening at The Anthem in Navy Yard, where Dutch sensation Joost Klein brings his world tour to the waterfront. If you saw his chaotic energy at Eurovision, you know the vibes are going to be high-octane and utterly unpredictable. If you prefer your Monday with a heavy dose of local flavor, head to Noma where Echostage is hosting the Big G Birthday Bash. Expect a masterclass in Go-Go beats that will remind you exactly why this city pulses the way it does.

Over on U Street, the options are almost overwhelming. 9:30 Club has the ethereal, genre-bending electronic sounds of ŁASZEWO, while DC9 is pulling double duty with indie-rockers Foxtide followed by the "Seven Nation Army" 2000s dance party for those who want to relive the blog-house era. If you’re looking for something more underground, Culture is hosting The Duplex with Simon Doty and Layla Benitez—a perfect choice for those who want their house music with a side of sophisticated grit and a very stylish crowd.

If your tastes lean toward the global, The Howard Theatre in Shaw is the only place to be. They’ve got the legendary Los Mirlos with Kristy La r for a night of psychedelic cumbia that’ll have the whole room moving in a tropical trance. Meanwhile, for those who just want to scream-sing 90s anthems until their vocal cords give out, White Ford Bronco is bringing their high-energy cover set to The Hamilton Live in Downtown. It’s nostalgic, it’s loud, and it’s exactly what a Monday needs.

Where to Go If You Want To...

Dance until the sun threatens to come up:
Flash in Shaw is your best bet, with Steller and Danny Daze bringing the techno heat that usually stays reserved for a Saturday at 2 AM. If you want a community-focused vibe with a killer soundtrack, Black Cat on U Street is hosting LUSH, the premier D.C. lesbian club night that always delivers on energy and inclusivity. Also, don't sleep on Decades in Dupont Circle for rita de cinco if you want that multi-level club experience.

Keep it casual (or a little classy):
For a more refined Monday, slide into a booth at Mr. Henry's on Capitol Hill for SoulJourners Jazz. It’s the perfect antidote to the high-decibel madness happening elsewhere. Alternatively, if you just want a world-class beer and a slightly nerdier, gear-focused crowd, the Brujos Bash at ChurchKey in Logan Circle is where the hop-heads will be congregating to talk shop and drink things you can't find anywhere else.

The fact that Ultrabar is running its "TGIF" program on a Monday night tells you everything you need to know about the stamina of Downtown right now. This mid-May surge is just a taste of the summer festival season starting to boil over, and if the city is this loud on a Monday, imagine what the coming weekend looks like.

Drink some water, call an Uber, and don't blame me when you're late for your 9 AM Zoom call tomorrow.
