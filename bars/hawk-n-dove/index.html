Hawk 'n' Dove

Hawk 'n' Dove

Category: Irish Pubs, Sports Bars, Karaoke Bars

Neighborhood: Capitol Hill

Price: $$

Address: 329 Pennsylvania Ave SE, Washington, DC 20003

Website
View on Google Maps →

✨ Vibe

A legendary Capitol Hill institution that blends polished tavern charm with an electric, high-energy atmosphere perfect for political insiders and sports fans alike.

🍸 Best For

Game days, political networking, team happy hours, late-night karaoke, and comforting American dinners.

🔥 Highlights

  • A Political Landmark: Boasting over 40 years of history and a legacy as a filming site for The West Wing, this historic venue features patriotic decor and salvaged memorabilia that celebrate its status as a D.C. staple.
  • The Ultimate Sports Spirit: Renowned as the premier destination for the DC Metro Auburn Club, the bar offers an unforgettable game-day experience filled with camaraderie, "War Eagle" spirit, and attentive service.
  • Elevated Pub Comforts: The menu shines with creative hits like the Spicy Pickle Brine Fried Chicken Sandwich, Hot Honey Pepperoni Pinsa, and fresh crabcake sandwiches that pair perfectly with their signature Hawk ‘N’ Stormy cocktail.

ℹ️ Need to Know

  • Reservations are available via Resy, making it a reliable choice for a post-work sit-down after advocating on the Hill or visiting nearby museums.
  • Don't miss the upstairs karaoke sessions, which offer an inclusive and welcoming environment for solo diners and large groups to unwind.

📰 In the News

HillRag

Stuart James Long (1942-2017): The Hill’s Restaurateur

Hawk 'n' Dove was founded in 1967 by Stuart James Long as a sanctuary on Capitol Hill where political rivals could gather over burgers and beer. This historic Washington D.C. tavern became a neighborhood staple and political watering hole, serving as a community hub for over four decades.
Roll Call

Hawk ’n’ Dove shuts down operations amid backlash

Hawk ’n’ Dove has shut down its operations in Washington D.C. following a period of significant public backlash. The closure marks the end of an era for the historic Capitol Hill establishment, which has long served as a prominent gathering spot for the local political community.
