Old Ebbitt Grill

Category: Oyster Bars, American, Historic

Price: $$$

Address: 675 15th St NW, Washington, DC 20005

Website
View on Google Maps →

✨ Vibe

A historic and charming downtown institution that blends timeless Victorian elegance with the high-energy atmosphere of a classic D.C. political power-spot.

🍸 Best For

Oyster enthusiasts, late-night cocktails, presidential history buffs, and quintessential D.C. celebratory dinners.

🔥 Highlights

  • The World-Class Raw Bar: Renowned for its "Oyster Eater’s Bill of Rights" and exclusive offerings like Great Kiss oysters, the venue is a premier destination for fresh shellfish and home to the legendary annual "Oyster Riot" competition.
  • Rich Presidential Heritage: As Washington’s oldest operational saloon, the antique-filled interior has hosted numerous U.S. presidents since 1856 and remains a central gathering place for major political milestones and inauguration celebrations.
  • Elevated American Classics: Beyond the seafood, the kitchen excels with signature Maryland crab cakes, award-winning crab dip, and fan-favorite desserts like their "divine" white chocolate bread pudding.

ℹ️ Need to Know

  • Reservations are Essential: Given its iconic status and proximity to the White House, this venue stays busy; booking ahead is highly recommended to avoid long wait times.
  • Daily Oyster Happy Hour: Visit between 3:00pm–5:00pm or 11:00pm–1:00am for exceptional daily discounts on raw bar platters and half-dozen oysters.

Old Ebbitt Grill is a historic Washington, D.C. saloon located near the White House, renowned for its classic wood-paneled interior and American Mid-Atlantic cuisine.
