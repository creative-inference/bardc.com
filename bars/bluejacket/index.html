Bluejacket

Bluejacket

Category: Breweries, Gastropubs, Sports Bars

Neighborhood: Navy Yard

Price: $$

Address: 300 Tingey St SE, Washington, DC 20003

Website
View on Google Maps →

✨ Vibe

A soaring, light-filled three-story gastropub set within a beautifully restored 1919 naval manufacturing plant that blends historic industrial charm with a vibrant neighborhood energy.

🍸 Best For

Pre-game Nationals Park festivities, craft beer exploration, casual group dinners, weekend brunch, and sports fans looking for an upscale atmosphere.

🔥 Highlights

  • An impressive rotation of 20 drafts and 5 cask ales crafted on-site, featuring unique flavor profiles like the "Forbidden Planet" and experimental barrel-aged rarities.
  • A modern American menu highlighting elevated comfort food, including their signature tomato soup and grilled cheese and artisanal seasonal brunch favorites.
  • The stunning "Boilermaker" architecture, which showcases the elaborate brewing system amidst original metal frames, expansive windows, and three levels of gathering space.

ℹ️ Need to Know

  • As a central hub for Navy Yard sports fans, reservations are highly recommended during Nationals game days and major sporting events.
  • You can take the experience home by visiting their on-site retail shop for exclusive, small-batch 16-ounce cans and fresh bottle releases.

📰 In the News

National Trust for Historic Preservation

Bluejacket Brewery in Washington, D.C.

Bluejacket Brewery is housed in the historic 1919 Boilermaker Building, a former naval manufacturing plant in Washington, D.C.’s Navy Yard neighborhood. The venue features an on-site brewery and restaurant called the Arsenal, offering 25 house-made beers and a modern American menu within an expansive, industrial-style space. The architecture preserves the site's history through original metal frames, expansive windows, and artifacts from its time as a boiler factory.
