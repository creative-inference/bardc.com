✨ Vibe

Zeba Bar is a lively and unpretentious neighborhood gem in Columbia Heights, known for its high-energy dance floors and relaxed multi-level lounge atmosphere.

🍸 Best For

Late-night dancing, group hookah sessions, affordable happy hours, and breezy patio drinks.

🔥 Highlights

  • High-Energy Dance Parties: Home to legendary events like the OMG BOYZ parties, where the music and atmosphere keep the crowd dancing all night long.
  • Excellent Hookah Service: Renowned for consistently smooth, high-quality shisha and a dedicated, spacious upstairs lounge perfect for social smoking.
  • Versatile Outdoor Space: Features a charming upper back patio that offers a great escape for enjoying frozen cocktails and snacks during the warmer months.

ℹ️ Need to Know

  • Take advantage of standout happy hour deals, including half-price wine bottles and great specials on bar bites.
  • The venue features multiple floors, with the upstairs area offering a more spacious, relaxed setting for groups and hookah enthusiasts.

Ex-D.C. Employees Charged In Five-Year Bribery Scheme To Erase Club Owners' Tax Debts

Zeba Bar's owner and operator, Davoud Jafari, was sentenced to 24 months in prison for his role in a five-year bribery scheme to erase the venue's tax debts. The scheme involved paying a District tax official to fraudulently reduce outstanding liabilities, contributing to nearly $1 million in total losses for the city.

Behind the Bars

Zeba Bar and Grill owner Davoud Jafari was sentenced to 24 months in prison following a conviction for his role in a bribery and tax fraud scheme. Jafari was found guilty of paying bribes to District officials to erase significant tax debts owed by the establishment.
WUSA9 Jan 25, 2024

Ex-DC nightclub owner found guilty of bribing officials to avoid paying taxes

Zeba Bar and Grill's former owner, Davoud Jafari, was found guilty in January 2024 of bribery, conspiracy, and wire fraud for a scheme to evade hundreds of thousands of dollars in District taxes. Between 2012 and 2017, Jafari bribed a D.C. tax official to falsify records and help the Columbia Heights establishment avoid tax collection efforts.
Thrillist Jul 7, 2011
