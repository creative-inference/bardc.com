✨ Vibe

Chicatana blends modern Mexican sophistication with a warm, family-rooted atmosphere, offering an authentic taste of Guerrero in a vibrant, newly expanded corner space in Columbia Heights.

🍸 Best For

Artisanal mezcal cocktails, adventurous fine dining, lively group celebrations, and fresh coastal Mexican flavors.

🔥 Highlights

  • Traditional meets contemporary with a menu featuring rare delicacies like chicatana ants and chapulines (grasshoppers) served alongside world-class handmade tortillas.
  • A standout seafood-centric program highlighting octopus sopes, scallop tostadas, and the signature Pescado Zarandeado.
  • An inventive beverage program headlined by the "Al Pastor" mezcal margarita and one of D.C.’s most curated selections of small-batch mezcals and tequilas.

ℹ️ Need to Know

  • Recently relocated to a significantly larger location featuring a beautiful covered patio and expanded bar seating to accommodate its growing popularity.
  • Authentic Mexican Happy Hour is available daily, and reservations are recommended for prime dinner hours.

Happy Hour

Chicatana offers special happy-hour food and great cocktails.

DC community raises funds for Petworth restaurant Chicatana after fire

Chicatana, a Mexican restaurant in D.C.'s Petworth neighborhood, is receiving support from the local community through a fundraising effort following a fire at the establishment. The initiative aims to assist the restaurant and its staff as they navigate the recovery process after the incident.
The Washington Post Jun 27, 2025

A fire destroyed this beloved restaurant’s kitchen — and its supply of ants

Chicatana, a Mexican restaurant on 14th Street NW, suffered significant damage after an early-morning fire destroyed its kitchen in June 2025. The establishment, which was previously named the best place to eat in D.C. by food critic Tom Sietsema, lost its specialized supply of chicatana ants in the blaze.
Washingtonian Jun 26, 2025

Fire Devastates One of DC’s Top Mexican Restaurants

Chicatana has temporarily closed following a significant kitchen and bar fire at its 14th Street location caused by an electrical issue with an ice machine. While the restaurant remains shut for the foreseeable future, its sister location, La Plaza, remains open and has added select menu items from Chicatana to support the staff and business.
