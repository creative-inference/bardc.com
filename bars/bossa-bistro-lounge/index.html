Bossa Bistro + Lounge

Bossa Bistro + Lounge

Category: Lounges, Live Music Venues, Latin

Neighborhood: Adams Morgan

Price: $$

Address: 2463 18th St NW, Washington, DC 20009

Website
View on Google Maps →

✨ Vibe

A soulful and high-energy Adams Morgan staple where authentic Brazilian culture meets an intimate art gallery setting that seamlessly transforms into a pulsing, late-night dance floor.

🍸 Best For

Live salsa and samba, craft caipirinhas, high-energy dancing, intimate dates, Brazilian tapas

🔥 Highlights

  • World-Class Live Music: Recognized by local musicians and critics as one of D.C.’s premier destinations for nightly live performances, ranging from traditional Bossa Nova and jazz to high-energy Latin ensembles like the Alfredo Mojica Group.
  • The Ultimate Dance Transformation: At 10:00 PM, the bistro sheds its tables to create a vibrant dance floor where guests can immerse themselves in salsa, samba, and cumbia beats.
  • Authentic Brazilian Flavors: An impressive menu of traditional tapas, including savory Camarão a baiana and a legendary flan that is widely considered a neighborhood favorite.

ℹ️ Need to Know

  • Arrive early for dinner to enjoy the Brazilian-inspired menu before the tables are removed for the late-night dance sessions.
  • The venue features two distinct levels, often hosting early jazz sets downstairs while DJs spin Latin hits upstairs on weekend nights.

Tadias Magazine

In DC, Ethiopian Jazz Supergroup Feedel Band Is Keeping Traditional Sounds Alive

Bossa Bistro + Lounge serves as the long-standing home for the Feedel Band, a premier Ethiopian jazz supergroup in Washington, D.C. The venue hosts the band's regular performances, which are essential to preserving and celebrating traditional Ethio-jazz sounds within the city's music scene.
The Kennedy Center

District of Raga

Bossa Bistro + Lounge serves as the host venue for the District of Raga concert series, which brings traditional Indian classical music to the heart of Adams Morgan. The venue provides an intimate setting for these cultural performances, solidifying its reputation as a hub for diverse global sounds in Washington, D.C.
