Martin's Tavern

Martin's Tavern

Category: Taverns, American, Historic

Neighborhood: Georgetown

Price: $$$

Address: 1264 Wisconsin Ave NW, Washington, DC 20007

Website
View on Google Maps →

✨ Vibe

A warm, historic Georgetown landmark featuring dark wooden interiors and stained glass that transport guests to a mid-century era of political legacy and cozy neighborhood charm.

🍸 Best For

Historic dates, weekend brunch, solo dining at the legendary bar, and soaking in D.C. political lore.

🔥 Highlights

  • Living History: Dine in the "Proposal Booth" where JFK famously asked Jackie to marry him, or sit in the same spots frequented by every U.S. President from Harry Truman to George W. Bush.
  • Classic American Fare: The menu celebrates 4th-generation family recipes, featuring favorites like the Ty Cobb salad, colonial Brunswick stew, and their renowned lobster risotto and pot roast.
  • Iconic Social Hub: Celebrated for its welcoming "mingling culture," it is the rare neighborhood spot where politicians, regulars, and visitors effortlessly fall into conversation over comfort food and cocktails.

ℹ️ Need to Know

  • Reservations are highly recommended for those wishing to experience specific historic booths.
  • The tavern offers an extensive late-night atmosphere, staying open until 2:30 AM on Friday and Saturday.

Atlas Obscura

The Presidential Booths at Martin's Tavern

Martin's Tavern is a historic Georgetown establishment renowned for its "presidential booths" where figures like JFK and Richard Nixon once dined. The pub is a destination for political history enthusiasts, famously featuring the booth where John F. Kennedy proposed to Jackie Bouvier in 1953. Guests can enjoy classic American fare and hearty pub favorites amidst a refined, white-tablecloth atmosphere.
Washington.org

Martin's Tavern, est. 1933

Martin's Tavern is a historic Georgetown institution established in 1933, famous for hosting nearly every U.S. President since Truman and serving as the site of JFK’s proposal to Jacqueline Bouvier. The restaurant offers classic American fare and local favorites in an atmosphere defined by historic architecture and traditional furnishings.
