Grand Central

Grand Central

Category: Sports Bars, Event Spaces, Neighborhood Bars

Neighborhood: Adams Morgan

Price: $$

Address: 2447 18th St NW, Washington, DC 20009

Website
View on Google Maps →

✨ Vibe

A high-energy, multi-level landmark in the heart of Adams Morgan where sports betting excitement meets a classic neighborhood party atmosphere.

🍸 Best For

Sports betting, milestone birthday celebrations, private events, late-night weekend dancing, and game-day gatherings.

🔥 Highlights

  • The Premier Sportsbook: As Washington DC’s first Class B sportsbook licensee, Grand Central offers a professional wagering experience with multiple kiosks and window service for all the big games.
  • Seamless Private Events: Specializing in large-scale social gatherings, the venue provides complimentary private spaces on its spacious second floor with flexible bar options tailored to your budget.
  • A Neighborhood Icon: Following a recent transition in operations after 17 years, this signature blue building remains a cornerstone of 18th Street, famous for its "Skip the Line" perks and vibrant music.

ℹ️ Need to Know

  • To bypass the weekend crowds, submit the "Skip the Line" form on their website before 8 PM on Friday or Saturday to enjoy immediate entry for you and a guest.
  • Grand Central is a top late-night destination in Adams Morgan, staying open until 3 AM on Friday and Saturday nights.

📰 In the News

thewash.org Oct 12, 2021

Grand Central bar in Adams Morgan marks its first week running a sportsbook

Grand Central in Adams Morgan became the first restaurant or bar in Washington D.C. to offer onsite sports wagering after obtaining a license from the District. The venue features a sportsbook as an entertainment offering, allowing patrons to place bets on professional and collegiate sports while dining and drinking.
PoPville Aug 31, 2021

Grand Central in Adams Morgan approved “for the operation of a full-service, independent sportsbook with no restrictions on maximum bets and all winnings can be redeemed on-site”

Grand Central in Adams Morgan operates a full-service, independent sportsbook as the first venue in D.C. to receive a Class B Operator license. Partnering with Elys Game Technology, the restaurant and bar offers on-site betting kiosks and terminals with no restrictions on maximum bets.
